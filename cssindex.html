The air Quality Index (AQI) is a number used by government agencies to communicate the daily air quality status and associated health risks in a simple, standardized way. It translates complex air quality data from various pollutants into a single number, a descriptor (e.g., "Good," "Unhealthy"), and a corresponding color code.
